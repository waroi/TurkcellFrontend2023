Radio and navigation codes, or anti-theft codes, are device-specific security or theft-deterrent access codes that are entered to unlock the radio and/or navigation system when these devices are disconnected from a power source (e.g., when the battery exhausts its charge and needs to be replaced).

American Honda and U.S. Honda dealerships cannot provide this code without the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) and serial number of the radio or navigation system, if equipped. The serial number can be located on a sticker or card in the glove compartment. If it is not available, some models support functionality to provide the serial number electronically. Please see this article for further details.

Once you have the VIN and device serial number, you may visit https://radio-navicode.honda.com to retrieve your codes. This complimentary service will require you to provide a U.S. address, email, and phone number.

Changing your Honda’s oil at the right time will help to keep your vehicle running smoothly. To help get the most out of your Honda, it has been equipped with a Maintenance Minder system.

The Maintenance Minder tracks the intervals between oil changes based on your driving habits. Once the “oil life” indicator reaches 15%, you’ll be notified each time you start the vehicle that you should consider changing the vehicle’s oil. It’s recommended that you have the oil changed by your local Honda dealership before the indicator reaches 0% to ensure the best performance.

If you have an older model Honda that does not come with a Maintenance Minder, you should follow the maintenance schedule included in your Owner’s Manual. An online version of the Owner’s Manual can be found here.

American Honda recommends using unleaded gasoline with a minimum pump octane of 87 or higher for most gasoline-powered Honda vehicles. For a few vehicles, American Honda recommends using unleaded premium gasoline, such as pump octane 91 for best performance. Use of gasoline with a minimum pump octane below 87 can lead to engine damage.

The Clarity Fuel Cell requires compressed hydrogen gas. To find the nearest hydrogen fueling station, refer to California Fuel Cell Partnership. The Civic GX (2015) requires compressed natural gas. To find the nearest natural gas fueling station, refer to the U.S. Department of Energy. Plug-in hybrid and battery-electric vehicles, such as the Accord Plug-In Hybrid, Clarity Plug-In Hybrid, Clarity BEV, and Fit EV can use an optional charging station to charge the high-voltage battery.

For more information regarding your vehicle’s fuel recommendation, review it in your Owner’s Manual here by selecting your vehicle’s year and model.
