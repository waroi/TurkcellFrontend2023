FAQS

To get priority in the space travel queue, you need to purchase a ticket and download our mobile application. The application will provide you with unique QR code that you need to scan to register for our loyalty program. The program awards points based on the number of flights you have taken and the duration of your stay in space. The more points you accumulate, the higher your priority will be for future space travel opportunities. The score information is kept confidential to protect our customers' privacy.

Yes, anyone can buy a ticket for space travel. However, you need to meet certain health and fitness requirements and pass rigorous training programs before you can fly to space. Before traveling to space, you need to undergo rigorous mental training programs that may include high-G training, microgravity training, and simulated spacewalks. You also need to learn about the spacecraft and its systems. You will be required to pass a medical examination to ensure that you are physically and mentally fit to travel to space.

The duration of a space trip depends on the destination and the purpose of the mission. It can range from a few minutes for suborbital flights to several months for orbital missions. The duration of a space trip also depends on the number of passengers and the amount of cargo that needs to be carried. For example, a suborbital flight to the International Space Station (ISS) may last for about 10 minutes, while a trip to the Moon may last for several days.
