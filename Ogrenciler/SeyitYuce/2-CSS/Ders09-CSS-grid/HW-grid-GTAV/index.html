Biker

Start your own Motorcycle Club (MC) and roam the open roads as a Biker. Tap into the criminal underbelly of Blaine County and Los Santos with a multitude of off-the-grid businesses, recruiting up to seven Prospects to help you turn a serious profit. Pushing product is just the start — work together to complete Clubhouse Contracts for clients, take on Club Challenges and Club Work, and gain access to benefits.

Getting Set Up

To start your journey toward becoming a Motorcycle Club President, acquire a Clubhouse through the Maze Bank Foreclosures website on your iFruit. Once you’ve chosen your Clubhouse’s location, you can customize it to your liking, choose a club emblem, and decide on a name for your MC. You can add a Gun Locker to your Clubhouse to customize your Weapon Loadouts, as well as a Custom Bike Shop to upgrade and customize your personal motorcycles. Once your Clubhouse is set up, register as an MC President by pulling up the Interaction Menu, navigating to Motorcycle Club, and selecting Start a Motorcycle Club.

The Clubhouse

Your Clubhouse can store up to 10 personal motorcycles and an additional seven rides for your MC members. As President, you can request any owned motorcycles to be delivered to you instantly in Freemode via the Motorcycle Club President section of the Interaction Menu. Each Clubhouse also contains a bar area where you and your members can get rowdy. Grab a drink from the bar, play some Darts, fire up the jukebox, or settle a score at the Arm Wrestling table. The Meeting Room is where you can bark orders at your Prospects and launch Clubhouse Contracts to take on some strong-arm consulting work for clients in need of muscle. In your office, you’ll find a laptop with access to The Open Road — this is where you’ll manage your various MC Businesses to make some serious green.

The MC

As MC President, you can manage your MC in the Motorcycle Club President section of the Interaction Menu, with options to change Riding Style, member Outfits, and more. Invite any player within your session to join your MC as a Prospect. You can then promote your members into various roles, each with their own unique abilities:

  • Vice President: The ability to drop Bull Shark Testosterone, a performance-enhancer that temporarily increases damage and toughness

  • Road Captain: Can call in vehicles and choose a formation to ride in

  • Sergeant-at-Arms: The ability to drop Molotovs and Ammo

  • Enforcer: The ability to call in a Hit Squad and drop Body Armor

Riding in Formation

Both Presidents and Road Captains can set a riding formation via the Interaction Menu. Riding in formation regenerates health, vehicle health, and armor all while traveling at the same speed as the formation leader. It also looks awesome.

Motorcycle Club Businesses

In your Clubhouse office, you’ll find a laptop that gives you unbridled access to The Open Road. This website allows you to acquire a number of illicit businesses. Once a business is stocked up on supplies, it will begin accumulating product over time. Keep a close eye on your businesses. You may sometimes be subject to police raids or other attacks, in which case you’ll need to defend your product. There are five businesses available, each with their own locations to choose from:

  • Weed Farm

  • Counterfeit Cash Factory

  • Document Forgery Office

  • Cocaine Lockup

  • Methamphetamine Lab

Once purchased and the initial setup mission is completed, ride over to your business and use its laptop to upgrade staff, equipment, and security. When you’re ready, trade your goods for a profit by launching a Sell Mission with your Prospects in tow.

Clubhouse Contracts

You can head to the meeting room within your Clubhouse to launch Clubhouse Contracts — Freemode missions for you and your MC members to earn some tidy GTA$ and RP through a range of noble deeds — from the forceful liberation of your falsely incarcerated allies to good old-fashioned gunrunning.

There are 11 missions total, but only three will be available at a time, rotating periodically.

Freemode Basics

Freemode is the live and open world of Los Santos and Blaine County. It's where you'll find everything from mission givers and businesses to social hubs and properties, and much more. Refer to the right-hand side of your Map in the Pause Menu to browse all available locations and set a Waypoint.

Using Your iFruit

Your iFruit is more than just a cell phone — it allows you to purchase Vehicles and Properties, quickly join available Jobs, reach crucial Contacts, summon assistance from Merryweather Security, and so on. Texts and Email are straightforward enough — these are messages from local persons, businesses, criminals, sometimes a combination of all the above.

Contacts

Your Contacts list contains all your known persons of interest, plus a couple of services that might be useful. Merryweather offers military and security services like Backup Helicopters, Ammo Drops, and Airstrikes — whatever you need to survive crossfire. If your Personal Vehicle is destroyed for any reason, you can call up Mors Mutual Insurance to order a replacement. Your Mechanic will bring you a vehicle from any of your Garages, so long as you’re roadside. Notorious criminal mastermind Lester Crest can remove your Wanted Level, take you Off the Radar, and more. You can request Contact Missions from various Contacts such as Martin Madrazo, Lamar Davis, or Gerald just by giving them a call.

Jobs

Jump straight into a selection of modes, Heists, Contact Missions, Jobs, and miscellaneous Activities using the Quick Join function. The Job List is where you’ll find invites to Jobs from your friends and fellow players. Snapmatic Snap a quick picture or selfie right on your iFruit. Once you’ve taken a picture, you can either Save to Gallery and your Rockstar Games Social Club account, or Delete it and try again.

Browsing the Web

Legendary Motorsport sells a range of high-performance vehicles. Southern San Andreas Super Autos sells an exclusive stock of motorcycles, sports vehicles, and more. Warstock Cache and Carry sells military vehicles to civilian clients. Elitás Travel sells private aircraft and rotorcraft. Docktease sells a variety of seafaring vehicles. Arena War — purchase and modify your own workshop and vehicles to ensure you smash the competition in Arena War. Benny’s Original Motorworks — Buy and modify a number of stock vehicles that can then be fitted with high-end mods and upgrades. Pedal and Metal — The premium store for cycling in San Andreas. Dynasty 8 — Purchase apartments and garage spaces in Los Santos and Blaine County. Dynasty 8 Executive — Specialists in luxury office space across Los Santos, including Agency properties. Maze Bank Foreclosures — Purchase Clubhouses, Bunkers, Hangars, Facilities, Nightclubs, Arcades, and Auto Shops. The Diamond Casino & Resort — Learn more about this luxury casino and resort in the heart of Vinewood and purchase a Penthouse. Maze Bank — Deposit cash in your Maze Bank account to keep it safe.

Local Points of Interest

The Diamond Casino & Resort — Stop by to play a few table games, spin the Lucky Wheel to win prizes including a chance to get the featured vehicle on the podium or shop exclusive fashion and designer artwork at the Casino Store.

Stores

Purchase (or steal) Snacks from Convenience Stores or change up your looks at any of the local clothing, tattoo, and barber shops.

Executive

Build your business empire from the ground up through the import and export of illicit commodities, take advantage of exclusive perks to elevate your status above the working-class denizens of the city, and create career opportunities for your friends along the way. This is how to succeed in business as a white-collar criminal in Los Santos.

Getting Started

  • Acquire an Office from the Dynasty 8 Executive website via your iFruit and start your business empire. You can also name your new Organization, customize the décor of your Office, and add useful amenities like a gun locker, a safe, and accommodations.

  • Once inside your Office, opening the Interaction Menu will allow you to make changes to the Office Decor or invite other players to your Office.

SecuroServ

Once you purchase your Office, registering to become a CEO will open many of the opportunities available to the modern Executive. To register, open the Interaction Menu and hit SecuroServ > Register as a CEO. Once registered as a CEO, the Interaction Menu can be used to open a variety of gameplay options via the SecuroServ CEO section, including:

  • Hire Associates: Hire other players into your Organization
  • Management: Choose your Associates’ clothing, dismiss employees, and more
  • VIP Work: Launch a variety of missions for your Organization to take on. These may require Associates, specific vehicles in your possession, or other CEOs in session
  • VIP Challenges: Competitive missions where Associates of your Organization compete against each other for GTA$ and RP
  • CEO Abilities: Use a selection of abilities to improve your Organization’s performance (for a nominal fee), such as bribing the authorities, or hiding you on the radar
  • CEO Vehicles: Request from a selection of vehicles to aid your Organization’s activities
  • Retire: Retire as a CEO
Among other perks, your Office Assistant can provide you with free Snacks to replenish your health during your next job. As an Associate, you can also use the SecuroServ section of the Interaction Menu to look for work and let potential CEOs know that you are available to support their activities. Just make sure you’re retired as a CEO and hit SecuroServ > Looking for Work > On via the Interaction Menu.

Making Money

Special Cargo
Acquire a warehouse and collect illicit contraband to eventually be sold off to a buyer for profit. Players can acquire crates via the Office desktop computer and sell those goods via the laptop located in the warehouse. The more goods stockpiled and sold in one transaction, the higher the price per crate.
Vehicle Cargo
Acquire a Vehicle Warehouse and source valuable cars via your Office desktop. These vehicles can be sold to buyers at a profit from your Vehicle Warehouse laptop. Selling up to four vehicles at once is a great way to earn more money faster. Your Vehicle Warehouse will also include an underground garage where you can store Special Vehicles bought from Warstock Cache & Carry, like the Ramp Buggy or Phantom Wedge.
Special Vehicle Work
Special Vehicle Work is unlocked on your Office desktop after completing a set number of Vehicle Cargo missions and can unlock discounts on the Special Vehicles featured in each mission. Special Vehicle Work requires two to four players.
VIP/CEO Work and Challenges
Open your Interaction Menu’s SecuroServ section to choose from a list of executive actions like Asset Recovery, Hostile Takeover, Piracy Prevention, and more to complete with the help of your hired Associates.

General Tips

Playing in an Organization rather than solo often yields higher GTA$ and RP bonuses, so team up if possible. You can hire Associates to your organization via the SecuroServ section of the Interaction Menu. Once registered as a CEO, hit SecuroServ CEO > Hire Associates. You can then send invites to:

  • Friends: Invite your friends to work for you
  • Player: A list of other players in session with you will be displayed for you to pick from
  • Rivals: You can hire a rival CEO, VIP, Associate, or Bodyguard
  • Nearby: Extend your offer of work to all available players nearby
  • Looking for Work: Offer work to players who are actively looking for gainful employment

Once your invites are sent, your prospective hires will need to accept the invite by pulling up the SecuroServ app on the bottom right of the iFruit menu. Hiring friends and other players as Associates is a great way to protect you from rival players and Organizations while you work. Be sure to check the workbench at your Cargo Warehouse and upgrade your delivery vehicles when you have the cash. If your vehicle is destroyed before you’re able to sell your cargo, you won’t see much of a return on your investments. Nightclub Owner

Nightclubs are the beating heart of Los Santos’ nightlife scene. Choose your Club’s location, build its popularity, pick resident DJs, and use your circle of influence to promote your spot as the best in town, all while disrupting your rivals and socializing with friends in the VIP section. Beyond the dance floor, your Club can also store other illicit goods and serve as an operations hub.

Setup

Visit the T icon on your map to chat with club scene legend Tony Prince and get started as a Nightclub mogul. From there, choose from ten different Nightclub locations on Maze Bank Foreclosures — each can be customized with lighting rigs, dry ice, and dancers to elevate the vibe of the space to your exact specifications. On the business end, there is also extra floor space available both for garage and storage for a variety of Goods types. There’s some setup to be done after purchasing a Nightclub and meeting with local nightlife impresario Tony Prince — namely picking up poached talent from other establishments and collecting necessary equipment. Once those are done and dusted, Solomun — your first DJ contact — will be ready for pickup from Los Santos International Airport.

Management, Popularity, and Upgrades

The computer terminals inside your upstairs office and in the basement handle all Nightclub operations. The Nightclub Management site monitors your Club’s current Popularity level, clientele, daily income, safe capacity, and celebrity appearances, among other stats. Managing your Club will help maintain and improve its popularity and status in Los Santos, mostly by promoting the spot and its resident DJs. Completing Club Management Missions in Freemode will keep your Popularity afloat, though some methods are more legally sound than others. The more popular your Club, the more packed it will become and the bigger your daily income will be. You can collect your passive income by visiting the hidden Safe located in the wall opposite your desk in the upstairs Office. You can make changes to your Club from these computers, too: Equipment, Staff, and Security upgrades will all help create a more formidable clubbing experience. Staff upgrades will diminish the rate at which your Club loses popularity, for example.

Dancing and DJs

Any Club worth its salt needs a selector behind the decks: a Resident DJ with name recognition will bring a throng of loyal fans to fill your dance floor around the clock. You can use your office computer to hire one of four Resident DJs — Solomun, Tale of Us, Dixon, or The Black Madonna — to set up shop behind the ones-and-twos. Once the DJ’s all set up, hit the dance floor with your friends and let loose. Your Club’s access levels, dress code, and more can be changed from the Nightclub Management tab in your Interaction Menu. Meanwhile, once you're cutting shapes, you can control the intensity, direction, and action of your dance moves using the menu in the top left corner of the screen.

Warehouse Management and Selling

Beneath the Nightclub, your Warehouse Technicians can passively accrue additional Goods from your Gunrunning, Biker or Special Cargo businesses. Accessing your Nightclub Warehouse is as easy as entering the elevator in the office or garage. Your Goods can be reviewed either in person or by using the computers in the basement and your Office. Hire additional Warehouse Technicians and assign them to specific types of Goods to prioritize certain businesses. Once you’ve amassed enough stock, complete delivery missions to turn a profit. The more Technicians you have, the more Goods you can accrue at once.

Terrorbyte and Delivery Vehicles

The Nightclub can also store the Benefactor Terrorbyte — a roving nerve center available for purchase from Warstock Cache & Carry that can be customized with a Drone Station, Weapons Workshop, and a specialized workshop dedicated to the customization of the Pegassi Oppressor Mk II. The Terrorbyte also contains a touchscreen computer that gives you access to Client Jobs — infiltrate LifeInvader with the drone to hack and secure data, disrupt alarm systems to steal precious jewels, steal from bank robbers, and more. Source and Resupply Missions from existing businesses can be launched from this location as well, giving you a single location to keep your operations in check.

Tips

Earn an extra 25% on your sales by looking out for Special Orders, which require a specific combination of different Goods types and quantities. Keep an eye out for Business Battles happening in Freemode which often award Goods for your Nightclub Warehouse. When delivering and selling Goods, a fast and tough vehicle is helpful in making sure your haul reaches its target destination intact. Customize your delivery vehicles with armor, weapons, and engine upgrades using your Nightclub’s Weaponized Vehicle Workshop.

What is GTA+?

GTA+ is Grand Theft Auto Online’s premium membership program, delivering a series of ongoing and rotating Member-exclusive benefits from across the entire GTA Online experience each month to PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S players. GTA+ Members will receive a monthly GTA$500,000 bonus, new property, vehicle, clothing, and event bonuses, discounts, and more — including a 15% increase on Shark Card values. Membership in GTA+ is completely optional and can be cancelled at any time. Memberships renew monthly, with new bonuses available to players to claim each month.

Checking Rewards

GTA+ Members can check out the available monthly rewards by opening the Pause Menu, navigating to the Online tab, and selecting GTA+ Membership. Alternatively, you can also view the latest GTA+ benefits here.

Property Benefits

Properties in GTA Online are more than just places to hang out, they can act as the launchpad for additional gameplay via missions, businesses, and opportunities for Heists that can prove very lucrative. Member-exclusive property-related benefits will be available for GTA+ Members to claim each month, such as a complimentary or heavily discounted property, property upgrades and more. Free or discounted properties can be claimed through in-game websites like Dynasty 8 Executive or Maze Bank Foreclosures. Use the browser on your iFruit phone in-game to access these websites. For some properties, you may also need to visit certain characters or locations first — check the Properties section of the GTA Online Guides for more info.

Vehicle Benefits

Member-exclusive benefits will be available for GTA+ Members to claim each month from across the entire spectrum of GTA Online vehicles, including everything from a free vehicle to a complimentary upgrade from the autotuning specialists at Hao’s Special Works, new vehicle liveries and more. Free and discounted vehicles can be claimed through in-game websites like Legendary Motorsport, Southern San Andreas Super Autos, Warstock Cache & Carry, DockTease, and Elitás Travel. Use the browser on your iFruit phone to access these websites. Vehicle customization and modification benefits can be claimed at in-game shops like any Los Santos Customs location, Benny's Original Motor Works in the Strawberry section of Los Santos, and Hao’s Special Works inside the LS Car Meet. GTA+ Members will see benefits listed in-game at a discounted price or for “FREE,” as applicable, each month. Visit the Vehicles section of the GTA Online Guides for more info.

Liveries

Liveries are custom paint jobs, decals or patterns that can be exclusive to specific vehicles. These can be claimed at in-game locations like Los Santos Customs, Benny's Original Motor Works, and Hao’s Special Works.

Clothing

Each month, new clothing will be added to GTA+ Members’ Wardrobe when logging in. Visit the changing room at any clothing store in Los Santos, or an owned property, to try them on. Add accessories for your character to wear by accessing your Interaction Menu and choosing Accessories from the Style section.

Upgrades and Rebates

Some GTA+ rewards may involve upgrades or rebates for vehicles or properties. This will usually require you to already own the vehicle/property to take advantage of the reward. Rebates will automatically be deposited directly into your Maze Bank account.

Gunrunner

Dominate the illegal arms trade in Southern San Andreas with Gunrunning: your career as a prolific arms dealer begins with the acquisition of a Bunker from Maze Bank Foreclosures. These underground fortresses will serve as your base of operations for all Gunrunning activities, and each one is equipped with a computer terminal to tap into the Disruption Logistics Network. This subterranean lair also comes with a suite of comforts and options. Once you’ve chosen your property from Maze Bank Foreclosures, you can pick from a range of styles. Add a shooting range (and compete against up to 3 other players), personal quarters, a gun locker, and bespoke transportation options.

Getting SetUp

To start the Setup mission, you’ll need to register yourself as a VIP or CEO or start a Motorcycle Club via the Interaction Menu. Once set up, source Supplies and assign your employees to manufacturing weapons, researching upgrades, or split their time between the two. Your stock will grow over time and when ready, reap the profits from your business by moving your merchandise to interested buyers across Southern San Andreas.

Research Projects and Using Supplies

Once you are set up, get your staff working on research projects across a range of branches, customization options for your Mobile Operations Center, upgrades for vehicles and weapons, and other goodies. You can also focus your staff’s attention on the production of arms. Both these endeavors require Supplies to complete, requiring you to resupply accordingly.

To Steal or Not to Steal

When it comes to supplying your Gunrunning business, there are two paths: stealing Supplies or buying them through legitimate means. Resupply missions are ways of procuring Supplies without having to fork over hard-earned cash. If money’s no issue, you can simply buy Supplies instead via the Disruption Logistics Network.

Stockpiling and Offloading

Your Bunker can hold 100 units of stock and once you’ve reached maximum capacity, you’ll need to offload. Selling weapons involves delivering sensitive cargo via the Disruption Logistics Network using delivery vehicles. When trying to sell large numbers of Crates, you may be required to deliver multiple vehicles. For these larger sales recruiting a team of up to four drivers might be the best course of action.

The Mobile Operations Center

For heavy-duty tactical operations on the move, arms dealers can invest in a Mobile Operations Center (or MOC for short), the kind of powerful support usually enjoyed only by trained assassins or rogue nuclear states. This drivable stronghold’s modular construction allows for tailored combinations of the Vehicle Workshop, Weapons Workshop, luxury living quarters, and Command Center — with facilities for upgrading and customizing your arsenal and modifying Weaponized Vehicles (including Turrets for your MOC that can be manned by your team) — all at your fingertips and pulled by a fully customizable commercial semi, with choice of cabs and interior color schemes. It’s easily storable in your Bunker, as well. To access your MOC from anywhere on the map, simply pull up your Interaction Menu and navigate to Services to request your MOC.

Mobile Operations Missions

After completing a few Resupply Missions, you can launch Mobile Operations Missions via the Command Center of your MOC. Each of these cooperative missions focuses on a unique Weaponized Vehicle, and completing missions will unlock discount purchase prices for certain vehicles at Warstock Cache & Carry.
